Research
Fabricated and tested polymer inclusion membranes for copper extraction from wastewater streams. Extraction rates of up to 49% have been achieved with the membranes.
Investigated the stability of polymer inclusion membranes. Current findings show instability of polymer inclusion membranes in aqueous environments over a given period.
Conducted ammonia gas removal and recovery experiments with two lab-scale hollow fiber gas membrane modules. Reduction of ammonia feed concentrations ranging between 400-1200 ppm with 0.5x1 MicroModuleTM and 40-1400 ppm with 1x5.5 MiniModuleTM was achieved.
Developed two mass transfer models to predict ammonia gas removal behavior in 0.5x1 MicroModuleTM and 1x5.5 MiniModuleTM respectively. Both models provide accurate predictions of outlet concentrations.
Developed a mass transfer model to predict the performance of industrial scale modules for treatment of ammonia-containing wastewater streams. The model provides reasonable insights into key parameters that affect ammonia transfer rates.
